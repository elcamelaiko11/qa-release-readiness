# Defect Triage & Prioritization Process

## Overview
Defect triage is conducted to review, classify, and prioritize reported issues to ensure efficient resolution and support release readiness decisions.

## Objectives
- Ensure critical defects are addressed before production release  
- Align defect prioritization with business impact  
- Improve communication between QA, Development, and Product teams  
- Support stable and predictable release cycles  

## Triage Participants
Typical triage sessions involve:

- QA Lead  
- Development Lead / Engineers  
- Product Owner or Business Representative  
- Project Manager / Scrum Master  

## Defect Evaluation Criteria

Each defect is reviewed based on:

- **Severity** – Technical impact of the issue  
- **Priority** – Urgency of fixing the issue  
- **Business Impact** – Effect on user workflows or revenue  
- **Reproducibility** – Frequency and consistency of occurrence  
- **Release Risk** – Potential to block deployment  

## Severity Classification

- **Critical** – System crash, data loss, or major workflow failure  
- **High** – Core functionality not working as expected  
- **Medium** – Partial functionality issue with workaround  
- **Low** – Minor UI or cosmetic issue  

## Triage Workflow

1. QA presents defect details and impact assessment  
2. Development team validates root cause and fix complexity  
3. Product team confirms business priority  
4. Team agrees on resolution timeline  
5. Defect status updated in tracking tool  

## Release Decision Support

- Open critical defects may result in release delay  
- High severity defects are evaluated for workaround feasibility  
- Medium and low defects may be deferred to future releases  

## Communication & Reporting

- Triage outcomes documented and shared with stakeholders  
- Defect trend reports monitored during regression cycles  
- Escalation performed for unresolved release blockers  

## Continuous Improvement

- Analyze recurring defect patterns  
- Strengthen test coverage for defect-prone areas  
- Improve collaboration between QA and Development  
- Refine prioritization guidelines based on past releases
